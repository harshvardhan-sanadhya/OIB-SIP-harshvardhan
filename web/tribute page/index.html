Swami Vivekananda

1863-1902

Talk to yourself once in a day,
otherwise, 
you may miss meeting an excellent person in this world.

Biography


He was a bright student and deeply interested in spirituality from a young age.
He became a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who shaped his spiritual vision.
Swami Vivekananda is best known for spreading Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world.
He gained international fame after his speech at the World Parliament of Religions in Chicago, 1893, 
beginning with “Sisters and Brothers of America.”
He emphasized the unity of all religions and service to humanity.
He founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 for social service and spiritual development.
Swami Vivekananda believed in youth power, self-confidence, and national pride.
He passed away on July 4, 1902, at the age of just 39.
His teachings continue to inspire millions around the world.
India celebrates his birthday as National Youth Day every year.
